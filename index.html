Your Essential Guide to Drop Off at Birmingham Airport

Planning a drop off Birmingham Airport (BHX) doesn't have to be stressful. Whether you're seeing off a loved one or catching your own flight, understanding the airport's drop-off system saves time, avoids fines, and ensures a smooth start to your journey. This guide, crafted with input from frequent travellers and local transport experts, provides clear, actionable advice aligned with Birmingham Airport's latest policies.

Birmingham Airport serves over 13 million passengers annually, making efficient terminal access critical. The dedicated drop off Birmingham airport zones are located directly outside each terminal, designed for quick passenger unloading. However, these are managed zones with time limits and charges knowing the rules prevents unexpected penalties.

Terminal Drop-Off Zones: What You Need to Know

Domestic & International Terminal (Main Terminal)

All scheduled flights operate from Birmingham Airport's single integrated terminal building. The drop off Birmingham airport forecourt is clearly signposted from the A45 and M42. Follow blue "Terminal Drop Off" signs. Vehicles can pull directly up to the kerb for passenger unloading. Important: this is a managed zone. As of 2026, the standard charge is £6 for up to 10 minutes. Payment is automated via ANPR cameras no tickets needed. Overstay beyond 10 minutes incurs additional fees (£12 for 10-15 mins, then escalating). Always allow buffer time for security queues after drop-off.

Accessibility & Special Assistance

Travellers requiring assistance should pre-book with their airline. Dedicated assisted drop-off bays are available closer to terminal entrances. Display your Blue Badge or assistance confirmation to airport staff for priority access. Birmingham Airport's team is trained to support passengers with reduced mobility arrive 15 minutes earlier than standard to ensure seamless help.

Peak Time Strategy

Between 4:00–8:00 AM and 4:00–7:00 PM on weekdays, the drop-off forecourt experiences high congestion. To avoid delays:

  • Use real-time traffic apps (Google Maps, Waze) en route
  • Consider dropping off at the Long Stay car park (free shuttle every 10 mins) if running early this avoids managed zone fees entirely
  • Pre-arrange meeting points: "I'll drop you at the main entrance kerb, Zone B" reduces confusion

Pricing, Payment & Smart Alternatives

Transparency matters. The official drop off Birmingham airport fee is £6 for the first 10 minutes. Payment is contactless or via the Birmingham Airport app. No cash accepted at barriers. Keep your vehicle moving idling incurs penalties. For longer goodbyes, use the Free Waiting Area (Cell Phone Lot) located 5 minutes from terminals. Wait here until your passenger is curbside-ready, then proceed to drop-off this avoids paying for idle time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to drop off at Birmingham Airport?

The standard charge for the managed drop-off zone is £6 for up to 10 minutes. Payment is automatic via number plate recognition. Always check the official Birmingham Airport website for real-time pricing updates.

Can I wait for free before dropping off?

Yes. Use the Free Waiting Area (Cell Phone Lot) located off Hangar Road. Wait here until your passenger is ready at the terminal, then proceed to the drop-off forecourt. This avoids paying for idle time in the managed zone.

What if my flight is delayed after drop-off?

If you've already dropped off and left, no action is needed. For pre-booked taxis, reputable firms track flights and adjust waiting times. Always provide your flight number when booking.

Are electric vehicles exempt from drop-off charges?

As of 2026, Birmingham Airport does not offer exemptions for EVs in the drop-off zone. All vehicles are subject to the same managed zone fees. However, EV charging points are available in long-stay car parks.
